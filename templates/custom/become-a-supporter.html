Pricing Tiers and Bands

Our fees are banded, based on country and size of institution, which means that scholars and institutions worldwide stand to benefit. Additionally, by supporting the OLH, you are also making knowledge available for the benefit of all, regardless of geographical location or financial means.

OLH annual memberships typically range from 900 and 2400 euros, which is similar to a single APC at many legacy publishers. We achieve this at reduced cost because we have our own in-house publishing platform that allows us to offer cutting-edge technology at a sustainable cost for our international library partners.

We also give the opportunity to voluntarily support us at a higher membership rate, tiered into bronze, silver, and gold levels. We have frozen our basic membership at below-inflationary rates to help libraries keep their costs under control in times of austerity. At the same time, we have opened the possibility of higher-tier support to libraries that can find the budget to expedite our programme for creating a more equitable publishing system.

Your investment

We are not a subscription publisher. We don’t charge fees for exclusive access to our journals. Instead, by joining OLH, you will help fund 28 open access journals in the humanities, making them accessible to anyone with an internet connection. You also become part of a collectively-funded, sustainable publishing ecosystem and a movement for change. By supporting us, you take a stand against exploitative commercial publishing practices and take a stand for progressive change in scholarly communications.

Standard level OLH membership sustains our publishing activities. In 2022, we published and funded a total of 469 articles across our 28 journals. These publications were also downloaded 1,122,354 times and viewed 2,539,747 times. They benefit from the latest advances in online journal publishing – with robust digital preservation and strong discoverability.

Choosing to become a higher supporter provides possibilities for the OLH’s expansion. We use these funds to flip more subscription journals to open access and making even more world-leading scholarship freely available to all. Flipping journals ultimately escalates pressure on other publishers to adopt models for equitable open access. This in turn allows greater knowledge sharing worldwide, saving libraries money in the process.

Governance and Advocacy

Librarians are our allies; in recognition, participating libraries are given a governance stake in the project. The OLH is governed by a Board of Trustees and three advisory boards (Academic Advisory Board, Library Board, and Publishing Technology Board). The Library Board is elected to represent our membership interests.

The OLH project is possible only because libraries are value-led organisations with an interest in maximising access. In 2018, we launched our EmpowOA programme to provide scholars and librarians working in the humanities with tools, spaces and cogent arguments to create a diamond open access future. Librarians can access a  toolkit that equips them to advocate for open access in the humanities, including videos, infographics, advocacy FAQs and reference sheets to strengthen the argument for an OLH membership or renewal. We also provide links to toolkits and resources from other open access organisations.

Badges

To thank all our members for their generous support, we provide digital supporter badges for all our institutions in recognition of their continuing commitment to supporting open access in the humanities. All our current supporting members are entitled to display a standard supporter badge on their institution's website, while higher tier supporters that sign up at our bronze, silver or gold rates are entitled to display one of our super-supporter badges in recognition of their extra contribution to open access publishing.

Other ways to support

A crucial way of providing a case for OLH membership is the use of our content by your scholars. We encourage you to recommend our 28 diamond open access journals as publication outlets to your PhD students and researchers and encourage the use of OLH articles in curricula. We also welcome expressions of interest to write for our Open Insights series of blog posts, which contain guest think pieces, interviews and interventions in humanities open access.
